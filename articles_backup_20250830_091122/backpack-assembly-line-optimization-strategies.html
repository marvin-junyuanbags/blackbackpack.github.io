üè≠ Lean Manufacturing Principles

Lean manufacturing forms the foundation of efficient assembly line operations, focusing on waste elimination and continuous improvement to maximize value creation.

Core Lean Principles

Value Stream Mapping

Identify and eliminate non-value-adding activities in the production process

  • Material flow analysis
  • Information flow optimization
  • Bottleneck identification
  • Cycle time reduction

Just-in-Time (JIT)

Produce exactly what is needed, when it's needed, in the right quantity

  • Inventory reduction
  • Pull-based production
  • Supplier integration
  • Demand-driven scheduling

Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)

Ongoing efforts to improve products, services, and processes

  • Employee suggestion systems
  • Regular process reviews
  • Performance optimization
  • Problem-solving methodologies

5S Methodology

Workplace organization system for efficiency and safety

  • Sort (Seiri) - Remove unnecessary items
  • Set in Order (Seiton) - Organize workspace
  • Shine (Seiso) - Clean and maintain
  • Standardize (Seiketsu) - Create standards
  • Sustain (Shitsuke) - Maintain discipline
