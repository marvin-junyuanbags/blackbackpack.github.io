Recycled and Upcycled Materials

Recycled materials form the cornerstone of sustainable backpack production, offering significant environmental benefits through waste reduction and resource conservation. Advanced recycling technologies enable the transformation of post-consumer and post-industrial waste into high-performance materials suitable for demanding applications.

Post-Consumer Recycled Materials

Recycled PET from Plastic Bottles

Source Material: Post-consumer plastic bottles transformed into high-performance polyester fibers

Processing Steps
1
Collection and Sorting

Plastic bottles collected from recycling programs and sorted by type and color

2
Cleaning and Preparation

Bottles cleaned, labels removed, and crushed into flakes

3
Melting and Pelletizing

Flakes melted and formed into pellets for fiber production

4
Fiber Production

Pellets extruded into continuous filaments and spun into yarn

Performance Characteristics
Strength: Comparable to virgin polyester
Durability: Excellent abrasion resistance
Moisture Management: Quick-drying properties
Color Retention: UV-stable dyeing
Environmental Impact
  • Reduces plastic waste in landfills and oceans
  • Saves 70% energy compared to virgin polyester production
  • Reduces CO2 emissions by 75%
  • Conserves petroleum resources
  • Supports circular economy principles
